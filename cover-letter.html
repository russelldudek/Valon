Applied AI transformation that survives contact with the work

Valon's New Ventures role is unusually direct: enter a client's operation, convert an ambiguous AI mandate into a sequenced execution plan, build the working solution, manage organizational change, and remain accountable when the workflow meets reality. That is not conventional consulting. It is embedded operating ownership.

My career has repeatedly placed me at that boundary. At Vape-Jet, I work across production, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, CRM, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows to turn distributed signals into visible work and cleaner handoffs. Through DudeWorth, I design agentic workflow patterns using structured context, retrieval, human judgment, escalation, evaluation, and governance rails. At Amazon and Compunetics, I learned to operate where customer commitments, technical complexity, quality, safety, and execution cannot be separated.

My point of view for the founding function is simple: build the workflow, then return the primitive. Each engagement should bind the business outcome to the real operator sequence, human authority, evidence standard, and recovery path. The client receives a working result; Valon receives reusable context, controls, evaluation, and a faster starting point for the next market.

Enter the operation

Observe real work, exceptions, incentives, and authority before choosing the tool.

Build directly

Use the smallest credible mix of workflow, agent, APIs, retrieval, rules, and human review.

Own and return

Operate to proof, recover safely, and package reusable learning for ValonOS.

I also understand the operating model is deliberately onsite. My background includes building a remote regional operation, leading regulated field activity, working directly with technical customers, and owning 24/7 execution. Significant travel and being the only Valon person in the room are not side conditions; they are part of the work.

The independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/Valon/ demonstrates this approach through a Field-to-Platform Buildline, a 90-day entry plan, and an engagement brief designed around the actual pressures of the role.

I would welcome the opportunity to work through a real client mandate, decompose it into an executable AI workflow, and show how I would decide what should remain client-specific versus compound into ValonOS.

Interview working session

  1. Decompose a real mandate
  2. Choose agent, workflow, or rules
  3. Bind authority, proof, and recovery
  4. Identify the ValonOS return

Carpe diem,
