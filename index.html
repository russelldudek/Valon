Why the role exists now

A founding function has to carry four contradictions at once.

The first engagements are signed. The strategist is embedded without a project manager or account executive, expected to build independently, manage change, and create the playbook that future hires will inherit.

01

Speed vs. auditability

Ship fast enough to change the business while preserving evidence, compliance, human authority, and recovery.

02

Client specificity vs. platform leverage

Go deep enough to solve the local operation without producing a one-off system that teaches ValonOS nothing.

03

Executive mandate vs. operator trust

Translate ambition into work people can understand, test, own, and improve without pretending fear is resistance.

04

Founder autonomy vs. repeatable method

Act with judgment in the room, then capture the decisions so the next engagement starts smarter.

Operating model

The Field-to-Platform Buildline

A five-stage discipline for turning an onsite mandate into a working, governed AI workflow and a reusable platform contribution.

01 / ENTER

Read the live operation

Observe the work, exceptions, data, incentives, fear, and informal authority before choosing a tool.

02 / FRAME

Bind outcome to authority

Name the business result, decision owner, human checkpoints, and failure cost.

03 / BUILD

Ship the smallest real workflow

Prototype with APIs, orchestration, prompts, tools, and operators - not a strategy deck.

04 / OPERATE

Own the outcome

Instrument quality, adoption, exceptions, economics, and recovery until the workflow works in practice.

05 / RETURN

Compound the context

Convert the proven pattern, evaluation, controls, and learning into Valon's platform and playbook.

First 90 days

Earn the right to standardize by shipping one complete engagement.

DAYS 1-15

Enter and map

  • Shadow the signed engagement.
  • Map work, authority, data, and fear.
  • Define the outcome and evidence contract.
DAYS 16-30

Build the first workflow

  • Select one high-value, bounded use case.
  • Prototype the agent/workflow directly.
  • Define human review and recovery.
DAYS 31-60

Operate to proof

  • Run with real operators.
  • Measure quality, exceptions, trust, and economics.
  • Iterate until the outcome exists.
DAYS 61-90

Return and repeat

  • Extract the reusable primitive.
  • Install the engagement playbook.
  • Define hiring and quality criteria for the next strategist.
Questions for the CEO / COO

Questions that expose the function Valon is actually building.

What has to be true for the first signed engagement to become a ValonOS capability rather than a successful one-off?
Which decisions can an embedded strategist make alone, and which require product, legal, compliance, or executive authority?
What failure at a client site would be commercially recoverable but strategically unacceptable?
How will Valon distinguish operator resistance from a legitimate signal that the workflow is wrong?
What evidence must a field-built agent produce before it can influence a regulated decision?
By the fifth engagement, what should be faster because this founding team captured context correctly?
